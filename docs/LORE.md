# Grovekeeper — Lore

This is a deliberately small file. The world of Grovekeeper has very little
plot, no canon characters with arcs, and no overarching narrative. What it
has is **tone** — and that's what content authors should reach into.

## The whole story

> You are The Gardener. The world holds groves. You find them and tend them.

Three sentences. That is the whole story.

## Who you are

The Gardener — singular, mythic, the only one. Not part of a guild, not
trained at an academy, not chosen by prophecy. You simply *are* The
Gardener, the way a river is a river. You walk, you tend, you build, you
keep walking.

You don't have a name in the lore, only the one the player gives you on
new game.

## What groves are

Pockets of consistent peace, scattered across an otherwise wild and
dangerous world. They glow. They sound different. They feel different.
They were always there, and they will always be there. You did not make
them — you find them and you make them yours.

A grove the player has not yet found is **undiscovered**. A grove the
player has stepped into but not lit is **discovered**. A grove with the
player's lit hearth is **claimed** — yours, fast-travelable, buildable.
The grove itself does not change. Only your relationship to it does.

## What grove spirits are

One per grove. They live there. They were there before you arrived and
they'll be there after you leave. Each one greets the gardener who comes
to claim its grove with a few words, then settles back into the ambient
business of being.

They are not gods. They are not bosses. They are not vendors. They are
**presences**.

## What the villagers are

Everyday people, peaceful, voxel-chibi shaped. They live in claimed
groves. They come from somewhere; they came when the hearth lit; that's
all the world tells you. They walk, they idle, they comment on the
weather and the season and the strangeness of having a Gardener around.

They do not give quests. They do not need rescuing. They are not in
distress. They are **alive**.

## What the wilderness is

The space between groves. It is biome-typed and infinite and varied.
Different biomes feel different — meadow is gold and warm, alpine is
sharp and white, scrub is rust and bone, wetland breathes. The wilderness
has its own life: deer, owls, herons, lizards, things that don't notice
you. It also has its dangers: wolves, bears, scorpions, blizzards. The
threat is real but not cruel. You can fight, or you can run home.

## What the hearth is

A small fire that says *yours*. The first thing The Gardener crafts. The
center of every claimed grove. Lighting it for the first time changes the
grove from a place the player visits into a place the player owns.

## Tone words for content authors

Use these. They describe the *feel* the game wants to maintain. NPC
dialogue, item descriptions, ambient audio, music, UI copy — anything
the player reads, hears, or feels — should land somewhere in this list.

- **Warm** — never cold-edge or sterile.
- **Reverent** — small fires in big quiet places.
- **Cozy without being saccharine** — sweet but earned.
- **Mythic without being overwrought** — small story, big resonance.
- **Patient** — the game is in no hurry. Sessions can be 3 minutes.
- **Curious** — the wilderness rewards looking around.
- **Companionable** — the villagers like you because you lit the hearth,
  not because they need you for something.
- **Alive** — even when nothing is happening, there are crickets, wind,
  distant chimes, the sigh of a tree settling.

## Tone words to avoid

- **Edgy / dark / grim.** This is not that game.
- **Twee / cutesy.** Warm reverence, not Pixar.
- **Heroic / chosen-one.** You are the Gardener. The world is fine
  without you, and it would have been fine without you. You are not
  saving anything. You are *tending*.
- **Urgent / ticking-clock.** No timer in the lore. No "before the
  darkness consumes us." The world has time for you.
- **Combat-glorifying.** Fighting is something that happens. It is not
  the point.
- **Quest-y / mission-y.** No "would you go fetch me." No "I need you to
  defeat." NPC dialogue should sound like *being there*, not *being
  used*.

## What is explicitly not in this world

- Plot.
- A villain.
- A prophecy.
- A doomed timeline.
- Lost knowledge to recover.
- A capital city.
- Other gardeners.
- Death.
- A god (the Grove Spirits are not gods).
- Magic systems with rules.
- A faction map.
- Anything that was in the older docs about *Wind Waker FPS, Daggerfall
  scale, eight spirits to collect, the Worldroot's Dream, the Birmother*.
  All of those were aspirational hallucinations from a previous spec
  iteration. They are not part of the world.

If a piece of content needs more lore than this file provides, the answer
is almost always *less plot, more atmosphere*. Reach for tone words, not
backstory.
